An overview of SenseS


People under high cognitive load are at risk of taking mental shortcuts, which can lead to poor decisions being made (tunnel vision effect, cognitive bias in behavioural economics). SenseS combines ThoughtWorks' capabilities in IoT, Agile Analytics and Experience Design to explore ways of helping people make better decisions by monitoring their cognitive load.


The primary goal of SenseS is to make workers aware of their cognitive load whilst in a safe educational setting such as a simulation exercise or a training program. All data recorded will be visualised as a training log which workers can then refer to in order to understand their personal mental load capacity. As a result, workers undergoing training will be more aware of the cognitive bias effect and hence take the right actions (e.g delay decisions, seek someone else's perspective etc.)



Technology Used


SenseS uses a wearable sensor to record GSR (Galvanic Skin Response) and correlate these values to levels of mental stress. An app is being developed to pair with the device via Bluetooth and provide simultaneous video of the subject wearing the device. The recorded video and stress levels can then be reviewed in real-time together to locate periods of high stress relating to an increase in cognitive load.


From here, the user can assess ways of improving their performance in these situations and visually identify actions/decisions that were influenced by their stress levels. The hope is that people who operate under high cognitive load such as pilots, surgeons and heavy machinery operators, can use SenseS as a training and educational tool to make their workplaces safer and more efficient.


User Testing


Through testing, it has been determined that the task load responses will be recorded using signals collected from the Shimmer Galvanic Skin Response and Optical Pulse Sensor on the wrist and fingers. Data will be streamed real-time to a Windows machine, with the outputs saved as CSV. The full collection period will be recorded so that participants can review their tasks and make annotations; in particular indicating their self-rating of the load experienced at each of the transitions.


There has been a lot of testing, and through doing this SenseS has provided a suitable platform for those in high-stress environments undergoing periods of high mental stress to enable operators to review their actions and level of mental load, and reflect on them.
