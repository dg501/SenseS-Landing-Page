Overview of SenseS


People under high cognitive load are at risk of taking mental shortcuts, which can lead to poor decisions being made. SenseS combines ThoughtWorks' capabilities in IoT, Agile Analytics and Experience Design to explore ways of helping people better understand the affect their mental load has on their performance.


The primary research hypothesis of the SenseS project is to explore the extent to which the awareness of the effect of mental load on performance is helping reflection and training. We are testing this hypothesis with flight instructors and pilots.



Technology Used

SenseS uses Shimmer to record GSR (Galvanic Skin Response) data and compute levels of mental load and an Android app (in development) to pair with the Shimmer device via Bluetooth to capture GSR data. The Android app provides a video of the activity the user engages with as well as the real time reading of their mental load.





Benefits


People operating in high stress environments can become more aware of how their cognitive load affects their decision-making and behaviour. Working with their instructor, they can use SenseS to identify areas of improvement and get personalised feedback.




Potential Applications


Pilots


Using SenseS's real-time display of cognitive load alongside a recorded video of the session, a pilot can identify how they may have prioritised certain tasks while neglected others and develop strategies to counteract any 'tunnel vision' effect adopted during the flight.


Drivers


Following a recording during an average drive, a driver can determine what maneuvers required the most cognitive effort and what events on the road caused them to make dangerous decisions.


Heavy Machinery Operators


In the training environment and accompanied by SenseS, a heavy machine operator can use the recording and cognitive load display to convey to operator what tasks caused them to react differently so they can improve their response.
